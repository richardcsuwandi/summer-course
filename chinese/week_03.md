# 🍜 Week 3: Food & Dining Out

## day 15

1.  订位 (dìngwèi)
    *   **pinyin:** dìngwèi
    *   **definition:** to book a table; to make a reservation (at a restaurant)
    *   **usage:** to arrange in advance to have a table available at a particular time in a restaurant.
    *   **example:** 我们最好提前订位，那家餐厅很受欢迎。(wǒmen zuìhǎo tíqián dìngwèi, nà jiā cāntīng hěn shòu huānyíng.) - we'd better book a table in advance, that restaurant is very popular.
    *   **contexts:** dining out, planning, restaurants.

2.  菜单 (càidān)
    *   **pinyin:** càidān
    *   **definition:** menu
    *   **usage:** a list of dishes available in a restaurant.
    *   **example:** 服务员，请给我们看一下菜单。(fúwùyuán, qǐng gěi wǒmen kàn yīxià càidān.) - waiter, please show us the menu.
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, ordering food.

3.  点菜 (diǎncài)
    *   **pinyin:** diǎncài
    *   **definition:** to order dishes (in a restaurant)
    *   **usage:** the act of choosing and requesting dishes from a menu.
    *   **example:** 你们想好点什么菜了吗？(nǐmen xiǎng hǎo diǎn shénme cài le ma?) - have you decided what dishes you'd like to order?
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, dining out.

4.  主食 (zhǔshí)
    *   **pinyin:** zhǔshí
    *   **definition:** staple food (e.g., rice, noodles)
    *   **usage:** foods that form the basis of a traditional diet in a particular culture, often grains or starchy vegetables.
    *   **example:** 你想吃米饭还是面条作为主食？(nǐ xiǎng chī mǐfàn háishì miàntiáo zuòwéi zhǔshí?) - would you like rice or noodles as your staple food?
    *   **contexts:** ordering food, discussing meals, nutrition.

5.  招牌菜 (zhāopáicài)
    *   **pinyin:** zhāopáicài
    *   **definition:** signature dish; house specialty
    *   **usage:** a dish that a restaurant or chef is particularly famous for.
    *   **example:** 这道北京烤鸭是他们的招牌菜。(zhè dào běijīng kǎoyā shì tāmen de zhāopáicài.) - this peking duck is their signature dish.
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, ordering food, recommendations.

6.  口味 (kǒuwèi)
    *   **pinyin:** kǒuwèi
    *   **definition:** taste; flavor (of food); a person's preference in food
    *   **usage:** the sensation of flavor perceived in the mouth and throat on contact with a substance; a person's liking for particular flavors.
    *   **example:** 这道菜的口味怎么样？(zhè dào cài de kǒuwèi zěnmeyàng?) - how is the taste of this dish? / 他的口味比较重。(tā de kǒuwèi bǐjiào zhòng.) - he prefers strongly flavored food.
    *   **contexts:** discussing food, cooking, personal preferences.

7.  清淡 (qīngdàn)
    *   **pinyin:** qīngdàn
    *   **definition:** light (of food, not greasy or strongly flavored)
    *   **usage:** describes food that is not oily, spicy, or rich.
    *   **example:** 我今天想吃点清淡的。(wǒ jīntiān xiǎng chī diǎn qīngdàn de.) - i want to eat something light today.
    *   **contexts:** ordering food, health, dietary preferences.

8.  麻辣 (málà)
    *   **pinyin:** málà
    *   **definition:** numbing and spicy (sichuan peppercorn and chili)
    *   **usage:** a characteristic flavor combination in sichuan cuisine, creating a sensation that is both numbing (from sichuan peppercorns) and spicy (from chili peppers).
    *   **example:** 你能吃麻辣的食物吗？(nǐ néng chī málà de shíwù ma?) - can you eat numbing and spicy food?
    *   **contexts:** sichuan cuisine, discussing flavors, ordering food.

9.  忌口 (jìkǒu)
    *   **pinyin:** jìkǒu
    *   **definition:** dietary restrictions; foods one avoids
    *   **usage:** to avoid certain foods due to health reasons, allergies, or personal preference.
    *   **example:** 我对海鲜忌口。(wǒ duì hǎixiān jìkǒu.) - i avoid seafood (due to allergy/preference).
    *   **contexts:** ordering food, health, allergies.

10. 买单 (mǎidān)
    *   **pinyin:** mǎidān
    *   **definition:** to pay the bill (in a restaurant)
    *   **usage:** the act of settling the bill for a meal.
    *   **example:** 服务员，买单！(fúwùyuán, mǎidān!) - waiter, the bill, please!
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, dining out.

## day 16

1.  AA制 (AA zhì)
    *   **pinyin:** AA zhì
    *   **definition:** to go dutch; to split the bill (literally "algebraic average system")
    *   **usage:** a system where each person pays for their own share of a bill, especially for a meal.
    *   **example:** 我们今天AA制吧。(wǒmen jīntiān AA zhì ba.) - let's go dutch today.
    *   **contexts:** dining out with friends, social etiquette.

2.  请客 (qǐngkè)
    *   **pinyin:** qǐngkè
    *   **definition:** to treat someone (to a meal, etc.); to host
    *   **usage:** to pay for someone else's meal or entertainment as a host or a treat.
    *   **example:** 今天我请客，大家随便点。(jīntiān wǒ qǐngkè, dàjiā suíbiàn diǎn.) - it's my treat today, everyone order whatever you like.
    *   **contexts:** social gatherings, business meals, celebrations.

3.  外卖 (wàimài)
    *   **pinyin:** wàimài
    *   **definition:** takeout; delivery (food)
    *   **usage:** food prepared by a restaurant to be eaten elsewhere, either picked up by the customer or delivered.
    *   **example:** 我今天太累了，我们叫外卖吧。(wǒ jīntiān tài lèi le, wǒmen jiào wàimài ba.) - i'm too tired today, let's order takeout.
    *   **contexts:** daily life, convenience, food services.

4.  自助餐 (zìzhùcān)
    *   **pinyin:** zìzhùcān
    *   **definition:** buffet; self-service meal
    *   **usage:** a meal where guests serve themselves from a variety of dishes set out on a table or sideboard.
    *   **example:** 这家酒店的自助早餐很丰盛。(zhè jiā jiǔdiàn de zìzhù zǎocān hěn fēngshèng.) - the buffet breakfast at this hotel is very rich.
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, hotels, dining options.

5.  小吃 (xiǎochī)
    *   **pinyin:** xiǎochī
    *   **definition:** snack; refreshment; street food
    *   **usage:** small, often savory, dishes that can be eaten between meals or as a light meal, frequently sold by street vendors.
    *   **example:** 夜市上有很多当地特色小吃。(yèshì shàng yǒu hěnduō dāngdì tèsè xiǎochī.) - there are many local specialty snacks at the night market.
    *   **contexts:** travel, food culture, casual eating.

6.  甜点 (tiándiǎn)
    *   **pinyin:** tiándiǎn
    *   **definition:** dessert
    *   **usage:** a sweet course eaten at the end of a meal.
    *   **example:** 你想来点什么甜点吗？(nǐ xiǎng lái diǎn shénme tiándiǎn ma?) - would you like some dessert?
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, meals, celebrations.

7.  饮料 (yǐnliào)
    *   **pinyin:** yǐnliào
    *   **definition:** beverage; drink
    *   **usage:** any type of drink except water.
    *   **example:** 请问您需要什么饮料？(qǐngwèn nín xūyào shénme yǐnliào?) - what kind of beverage would you like?
    *   **contexts:** ordering in restaurants, cafes, daily life.

8.  开胃菜 (kāiwèicài)
    *   **pinyin:** kāiwèicài
    *   **definition:** appetizer; starter
    *   **usage:** a small dish of food or a drink taken before a meal or the main course of a meal to stimulate one's appetite.
    *   **example:** 我们先点几道开胃菜吧。(wǒmen xiān diǎn jǐ dào kāiwèicài ba.) - let's order a few appetizers first.
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, formal dining, meals.

9.  汤 (tāng)
    *   **pinyin:** tāng
    *   **definition:** soup
    *   **usage:** a liquid dish, typically made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables with stock or water.
    *   **example:** 冬天喝碗热汤很舒服。(dōngtiān hē wǎn rètāng hěn shūfu.) - drinking a bowl of hot soup is very comforting in winter.
    *   **contexts:** meals, Chinese cuisine, health.

10. 素食 (sùshí)
    *   **pinyin:** sùshí
    *   **definition:** vegetarian food; vegetarian diet
    *   **usage:** food that does not include meat or fish.
    *   **example:** 这家餐厅提供素食选择吗？(zhè jiā cāntīng tígōng sùshí xuǎnzé ma?) - does this restaurant offer vegetarian options?
    *   **contexts:** dietary preferences, health, restaurants.

## day 17

1.  食材 (shícái)
    *   **pinyin:** shícái
    *   **definition:** ingredients (for food)
    *   **usage:** any of the foods or substances that are combined to make a particular dish.
    *   **example:** 这道菜用的食材都很新鲜。(zhè dào cài yòng de shícái dōu hěn xīnxiān.) - the ingredients used in this dish are all very fresh.
    *   **contexts:** cooking, recipes, food shopping, restaurants.

2.  新鲜 (xīnxiān)
    *   **pinyin:** xīnxiān
    *   **definition:** fresh (of food, air, etc.)
    *   **usage:** (of food) recently made or obtained; not canned, frozen, or otherwise preserved.
    *   **example:** 市场上的蔬菜很新鲜。(shìchǎng shàng de shūcài hěn xīnxiān.) - the vegetables in the market are very fresh.
    *   **contexts:** food, cooking, shopping, environment.

3.  有机 (yǒujī)
    *   **pinyin:** yǒujī
    *   **definition:** organic (food)
    *   **usage:** produced or involving production without the use of chemical fertilizers, pesticides, or other artificial agents.
    *   **example:** 我更喜欢买有机蔬菜。(wǒ gèng xǐhuān mǎi yǒujī shūcài.) - i prefer to buy organic vegetables.
    *   **contexts:** food, health, agriculture, shopping.

4.  营养 (yíngyǎng)
    *   **pinyin:** yíngyǎng
    *   **definition:** nutrition; nourishment
    *   **usage:** the process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth.
    *   **example:** 这顿饭营养很均衡。(zhè dùn fàn yíngyǎng hěn jūnhéng.) - this meal is very well-balanced in nutrition.
    *   **contexts:** health, food, diet, medicine.

5.  卡路里 (kǎlùlǐ)
    *   **pinyin:** kǎlùlǐ
    *   **definition:** calorie
    *   **usage:** a unit of energy, often used to measure the energy content of food and drinks.
    *   **example:** 这块蛋糕的卡路里很高。(zhè kuài dàngāo de kǎlùlǐ hěn gāo.) - this piece of cake is high in calories.
    *   **contexts:** health, diet, fitness, food labels.

6.  味道 (wèidào)
    *   **pinyin:** wèidào
    *   **definition:** taste; flavor; smell
    *   **usage:** the sensation of flavor perceived in the mouth and throat on contact with a substance; a distinctive smell, especially an unpleasant one.
    *   **example:** 这汤味道不错。(zhè tāng wèidào bùcuò.) - this soup tastes good. / 你闻到什么味道了吗？(nǐ wéndào shénme wèidào le ma?) - do you smell anything?
    *   **contexts:** food, cooking, senses.

7.  香 (xiāng)
    *   **pinyin:** xiāng
    *   **definition:** fragrant; delicious; aromatic
    *   **usage:** having a pleasant, sweet smell; (of food) appetizing.
    *   **example:** 刚出炉的面包真香！(gāng chūlú de miànbāo zhēn xiāng!) - the freshly baked bread smells so good!
    *   **contexts:** food, cooking, flowers, perfumes.

8.  臭 (chòu)
    *   **pinyin:** chòu
    *   **definition:** smelly; stinky; foul
    *   **usage:** having a strong, unpleasant smell.
    *   **example:** 这种奶酪闻起来有点臭，但吃起来很香。(zhè zhǒng nǎilào wén qǐlái yǒudiǎn chòu, dàn chī qǐlái hěn xiāng.) - this cheese smells a bit stinky, but it tastes delicious (e.g. stinky tofu, blue cheese).
    *   **contexts:** food (e.g. stinky tofu), hygiene, environment.

9.  酸 (suān)
    *   **pinyin:** suān
    *   **definition:** sour; tart
    *   **usage:** having an acid taste like lemon or vinegar.
    *   **example:** 这个柠檬太酸了。(zhège níngméng tài suān le.) - this lemon is too sour.
    *   **contexts:** food, flavors, fruits.

10. 甜 (tián)
    *   **pinyin:** tián
    *   **definition:** sweet
    *   **usage:** having the pleasant taste characteristic of sugar or honey; not salty, sour, or bitter.
    *   **example:** 我喜欢吃甜的。(wǒ xǐhuān chī tián de.) - i like to eat sweet things.
    *   **contexts:** food, flavors, desserts.

## day 18

1.  苦 (kǔ)
    *   **pinyin:** kǔ
    *   **definition:** bitter
    *   **usage:** having a sharp, pungent taste or smell; not sweet.
    *   **example:** 这杯咖啡有点苦。(zhè bēi kāfēi yǒudiǎn kǔ.) - this cup of coffee is a bit bitter.
    *   **contexts:** food (coffee, bitter melon), medicine, life experiences (hardship).

2.  辣 (là)
    *   **pinyin:** là
    *   **definition:** spicy; hot (flavor)
    *   **usage:** having a strong, hot flavor from chili peppers.
    *   **example:** 四川菜以辣闻名。(sìchuāncài yǐ là wénmíng.) - sichuan cuisine is famous for its spiciness.
    *   **contexts:** food, flavors, cuisine types (sichuan, hunan).

3.  咸 (xián)
    *   **pinyin:** xián
    *   **definition:** salty
    *   **usage:** tasting of, containing, or preserved with salt.
    *   **example:** 这个汤有点咸了。(zhège tāng yǒudiǎn xián le.) - this soup is a bit salty.
    *   **contexts:** food, flavors, cooking.

4.  淡 (dàn)
    *   **pinyin:** dàn
    *   **definition:** bland; tasteless; light (in color or flavor)
    *   **usage:** lacking strong flavor; (of color) not strong or dark.
    *   **example:** 我觉得这个菜味道有点淡。(wǒ juéde zhège cài wèidào yǒudiǎn dàn.) - i think this dish is a bit bland.
    *   **contexts:** food, flavors, colors.

5.  油腻 (yóunì)
    *   **pinyin:** yóunì
    *   **definition:** greasy; oily (food)
    *   **usage:** containing or covered with a lot of oil or grease.
    *   **example:** 我不喜欢吃太油腻的食物。(wǒ bù xǐhuān chī tài yóunì de shíwù.) - i don't like to eat food that is too greasy.
    *   **contexts:** food, cooking, health.

6.  脆 (cuì)
    *   **pinyin:** cuì
    *   **definition:** crispy; crunchy
    *   **usage:** firm and making a sharp sound when broken or bitten.
    *   **example:** 这个苹果很脆。(zhège píngguǒ hěn cuì.) - this apple is very crispy.
    *   **contexts:** food texture, snacks, fruits, vegetables.

7.  嫩 (nèn)
    *   **pinyin:** nèn
    *   **definition:** tender (of meat, vegetables, etc.); young
    *   **usage:** (of food) easy to cut or chew; not tough. (of a person or plant) young and vulnerable.
    *   **example:** 这块牛排很嫩。(zhè kuài niúpái hěn nèn.) - this steak is very tender.
    *   **contexts:** food texture (meat, vegetables), age (young).

8.  老 (lǎo)
    *   **pinyin:** lǎo
    *   **definition:** tough (of meat); old (opposite of嫩 for food texture)
    *   **usage:** (of meat) difficult to chew or cut. also means old in general.
    *   **example:** 这个鸡肉有点老。(zhège jīròu yǒudiǎn lǎo.) - this chicken is a bit tough.
    *   **contexts:** food texture (meat), age.

9.  烫 (tàng)
    *   **pinyin:** tàng
    *   **definition:** scalding hot; to burn (oneself); to scald; to iron
    *   **usage:** so hot as to burn or scald. can also mean to iron clothes.
    *   **example:** 小心，汤很烫！(xiǎoxīn, tāng hěn tàng!) - be careful, the soup is very hot!
    *   **contexts:** food temperature, safety, household chores (ironing).

10. 冰镇 (bīngzhèn)
    *   **pinyin:** bīngzhèn
    *   **definition:** iced; chilled
    *   **usage:** cooled with ice or in a refrigerator.
    *   **example:** 夏天我喜欢喝冰镇饮料。(xiàtiān wǒ xǐhuān hē bīngzhèn yǐnliào.) - i like to drink iced beverages in the summer.
    *   **contexts:** drinks, food temperature, summer.

## day 19

1.  一日三餐 (yī rì sān cān)
    *   **pinyin:** yī rì sān cān
    *   **definition:** three meals a day
    *   **usage:** refers to the typical eating schedule of breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
    *   **example:** 保证一日三餐的规律很重要。(bǎozhèng yī rì sān cān de guīlǜ hěn zhòngyào.) - it's important to ensure the regularity of three meals a day.
    *   **contexts:** daily routines, health, diet.

2.  早餐 (zǎocān)
    *   **pinyin:** zǎocān
    *   **definition:** breakfast
    *   **usage:** the first meal of the day.
    *   **example:** 你今天早上吃早餐了吗？(nǐ jīntiān zǎoshang chī zǎocān le ma?) - did you eat breakfast this morning?
    *   **contexts:** daily routines, meals.

3.  午餐 (wǔcān)
    *   **pinyin:** wǔcān
    *   **definition:** lunch
    *   **usage:** a meal eaten in the middle of the day.

4.  晚餐 (wǎncān)

    *   **definition:** dinner; supper
    *   **usage:** the main meal of the day, taken either in the evening or midday.

5.  夜宵 (yèxiāo)

    *   **contexts:** casual conversation, dining out.

7.  叫外卖 (jiào wàimài)
    *   **pinyin:** jiào wàimài
    *   **definition:** to order takeout/delivery
    *   **usage:** to request food to be delivered from a restaurant.
    *   **example:** 如果不想出门，我们可以叫外卖。(rúguǒ bù xiǎng chūmén, wǒmen kěyǐ jiào wàimài.) - if we don't want to go out, we can order delivery.
    *   **contexts:** daily life, convenience, food services.

8.  打包 (dǎbāo)
    *   **pinyin:** dǎbāo
    *   **definition:** to pack (leftovers); to get a doggy bag
    *   **usage:** to put leftover food from a restaurant meal into a container to take home.
    *   **example:** 服务员，这些菜吃不完，麻烦帮我打包。(fúwùyuán, zhèxiē cài chī bù wán, máfan bāng wǒ dǎbāo.) - waiter, we can't finish these dishes, please pack them up for us.

9.  厨房 (chúfáng)
    *   **pinyin:** chúfáng
    *   **definition:** kitchen
    *   **usage:** a room or area where food is prepared and cooked.
    *   **example:** 我妈妈在厨房里做饭。(wǒ māma zài chúfáng lǐ zuòfàn.) - my mom is cooking in the kitchen.
    *   **contexts:** home, cooking, restaurants.

10. 厨师 (chúshī)
    *   **pinyin:** chúshī
    *   **definition:** chef; cook
    *   **usage:** a professional cook, typically the chief cook in a restaurant or hotel.
    *   **example:** 这家餐厅的厨师手艺很好。(zhè jiā cāntīng de chúshī shǒuyì hěn hǎo.) - the chef at this restaurant is very skilled.
    *   **contexts:** restaurants, cooking, professions.

## day 20

1.  烹饪技巧 (pēngrèn jìqiǎo)
    *   **pinyin:** pēngrèn jìqiǎo
    *   **definition:** cooking skills; culinary techniques
    *   **usage:** methods and abilities used in preparing and cooking food.
    *   **example:** 她想学习更多的烹饪技巧。(tā xiǎng xuéxí gèng duō de pēngrèn jìqiǎo.) - she wants to learn more cooking techniques.
    *   **contexts:** cooking, education, hobbies, professional skills.

2.  炒 (chǎo)
    *   **pinyin:** chǎo
    *   **definition:** to stir-fry
    *   **usage:** a chinese cooking technique in which ingredients are fried in a small amount of very hot oil while being stirred or tossed in a wok.
    *   **example:** 我会炒鸡蛋。(wǒ huì chǎo jīdàn.) - i can stir-fry eggs.
    *   **contexts:** cooking, chinese cuisine, recipes.

3.  蒸 (zhēng)
    *   **pinyin:** zhēng
    *   **definition:** to steam
    *   **usage:** to cook (food) by heating it in steam from boiling water.
    *   **example:** 清蒸鱼很健康。(qīngzhēng yú hěn jiànkāng.) - steamed fish is very healthy.
    *   **contexts:** cooking, chinese cuisine, healthy eating, recipes.

4.  煮 (zhǔ)
    *   **pinyin:** zhǔ
    *   **definition:** to boil; to cook (in water)
    *   **usage:** to cook (food) in boiling water or other water-based liquids such as stock or milk.
    *   **example:** 你会煮面条吗？(nǐ huì zhǔ miàntiáo ma?) - can you boil noodles?
    *   **contexts:** cooking, recipes.

5.  炸 (zhá)
    *   **pinyin:** zhá
